Bio

Hello! My name is Raanan. I was born in New York City, raised in Baltimore and have been living in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania since 2015. I am the founder and president of Geominr LLC, which provides data analytics and web development consulting services. I founded Geominr with the mission to make Open Data more accessible to and actionable for everyone, regardless of technical, industrial or educational background.

I had the idea while working at the US Census Bureau in 2019, having spent the summer after my graduation immersed in the world of Civic Tech. It was in college, however, when I realized that data could have an immediate and powerful impact on the lives of everyday people. My sophomore year I started a tap water testing service for students at University of Pittsburgh concerned about lead in their water. The goal was to empower individuals with information about their own water quality, while also helping to understand who was at risk and who was not. In the end, we signed up over 100 students and local residents to recieve free water tests and filters and I found a passion project that would immerse me in the fields of geoinformatics, machine learning and public health.

When I am not working, you can usually find me on the trails riding my bike or hiking, taking care of my house plants or swinging on my hammock. Before Covid-19, I was an avid traveller, having visited over 24 countries on 4 continents for almost 16 total months of my life... and (after Covid) counting! I also enjoy reading/listening to books about history, psychology, philosophy of mind and science fiction.

Research

My research in the Geoinformatics Lab and Public Health Dynamics Lab has focused on applications of geoinformatics in health and urban systems, including studies of the Covid-19 pandemic, safe drinking water and accessible navigation.
